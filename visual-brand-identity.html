Housing Policy Research · May 2026

Center on Budget and Policy Priorities

Funding Limitations Cause Large Unmet Need for Federal Rental Assistance

Only 1 in 4 eligible low-income households receive federal rental assistance — funding constraints, not eligibility rules, determine who gets help

By: Erik Gartland

Millions of eligible families remain on waitlists or receive no assistance due to chronic underfunding.

Federal rental assistance programs — including Housing Choice Vouchers and project-based Section 8 — reach only a fraction of the households that qualify.1 Unlike entitlement programs such as Medicaid, rental assistance is funded through annual discretionary appropriations, creating a persistent gap between need and availability. Millions of eligible low-income families remain on waitlists for years or forgo assistance entirely, contributing to housing instability, overcrowding, and homelessness across the country.

Endnotes
  1. Erik Gartland, "Funding Limitations Create Widespread Unmet Need for Rental Assistance," Center on Budget and Policy Priorities, February 2022. cbpp.org/research/housing/funding-limitations-create-widespread-unmet-need-for-rental-assistance
